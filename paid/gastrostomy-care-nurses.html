What is Gastrostomy?

Gastrostomy is a medical procedure that involves creating an opening in the abdomen to allow for the insertion of a feeding tube directly into the stomach. This procedure is typically performed for individuals who have difficulty swallowing or cannot consume food orally due to various medical conditions, such as neurological disorders, head and neck cancers, or severe injuries.

The feeding tube, known as a gastrostomy tube or G-tube, allows for the delivery of nutrition, fluids, and medications directly into the stomach, bypassing the need for oral intake. Gastrostomy care involves regular maintenance of the feeding tube, monitoring for any signs of infection or complications, and ensuring that the patient receives proper nutrition and hydration through the tube.
