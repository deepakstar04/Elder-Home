Helpful hints

  • Eat a well-balanced diet including vegetables and fresh fruits. Eat on a regular schedule. Drink at least 6 to 8 glasses of fluids daily.
  • Eat slowly in a relaxed atmosphere. Chew your food thoroughly. Avoid chewing gum, smoking, and drinking from a straw. This will help decrease the amount of air you swallow, which may help reduce gas.
  • Regularly clean the stoma and surrounding skin to prevent irritation and infection.
  • Use a stoma guard or barrier cream to protect the skin from leakage and irritation.
  • Ensure that the colostomy bag is securely attached and emptied regularly to prevent leaks and discomfort.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote overall well-being.
  • Seek support from healthcare professionals, support groups, or counseling services to address emotional and psychological challenges associated with living with a colostomy.

Applying the pouching system

The colostomy pouching system is designed to securely collect waste from the stoma while protecting the skin around it. To apply the pouching system, follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before handling the pouching system.
  2. Clean the stoma and surrounding skin with warm water and mild soap, then pat dry with a clean towel.
  3. Measure the stoma size using a stoma measuring guide to ensure a proper fit for the pouching system.
  4. Cut the opening of the pouching system to match the size of the stoma, leaving a small gap for comfort.
  5. Apply a skin barrier or adhesive paste around the stoma to protect the skin and enhance adhesion.
  6. Position the pouching system over the stoma, ensuring that it is securely attached and properly aligned.
  7. Press down firmly around the edges of the pouching system to ensure a secure seal and prevent leaks.
  8. Empty the pouch regularly when it is about one-third full to prevent leaks and discomfort.

Reducing Odor

To reduce odor from your colostomy bag, consider the following tips:

  • Use an odor-neutralizing filter or deodorizer specifically designed for colostomy bags.
  • Empty the pouch regularly to prevent waste from sitting too long and producing odor.
  • Consider using a carbon filter or activated charcoal in your pouching system to absorb odors.
  • Maintain good hygiene by washing the stoma and surrounding skin regularly with warm water and mild soap.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet to promote overall well-being and reduce odor production.
